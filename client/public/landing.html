The honest standard

She grades her own evidence — and tells you which is which.

Confidence without the sleight of hand. Every flag carries the reason it’s flagged — settled science, a real-but-unsettled signal, or Kristy’s own standard, named as such.

These are how I grade the evidence — the verdict is what I tell you to do about it.

Established

Strong scientific consensus, a major health-organization classification, or regulatory action. Settled enough to act on without debate.

Sodium nitrite Skip

The curing agent in deli meats, hot dogs, and bacon. IARC classifies nitrite-cured processed meat as a Group 1 carcinogen.

Settled science
Credible concern

Meaningful peer-reviewed evidence of harm. The debate’s ongoing, but the signal is real — worth flagging, especially when a clean swap exists.

Soybean oil Skip

~54% linoleic acid, among the highest of any common oil. UC Riverside research tied it to metabolic and neurological effects in animals.

Real, but unsettled
Kristy’s standard

Her whole-food philosophy, even where regulators haven’t acted. She’ll always say when it’s her standard, not settled science — and she stands behind it.

Canola oil Skip

Solvent-extracted and stripped by high heat — that processing oxidizes the oil, and oxidized fats are the real problem.

Flagged on my standard

Certainty when it’s earned, honesty when it isn’t. That’s what makes the confidence worth trusting.
